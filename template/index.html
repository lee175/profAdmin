Department of Mechanical Engineering
Professor

Atul Srivastava

Time-dependent and three dimensional distribution of fluid velocities and transported properties such as temperature and species concentration are required in a range of applications. However, generating such voluminous data through experiments has always been a challenge. Conventional probe-based measurement techniques are intrusive in nature and are restricted to providing volume-averaged data only at a few selected points. Time constants associated with such probes limit their applications in highly transient and short-lived phenomena. The primary focus of our research group is to overcome this barrier of three dimensional unsteady whole field measurements using advanced measurement techniques that are completely inertia-free and non-intrusive in nature. These experimental measurements are complemented by numerical simulations performed using in-house developed codes as well as through commercially available software, for instance, Fluent. Some of the important areas of fluids and thermal sciences wherein the group has made significant impact include nanofluids, two-phase heat transfer (nucleate pool and flow boiling), droplets, bio-heat transfer and crystal growth.
